What is tap to pay?

Tap to pay (contactless) lets you pay by holding a card, phone or wearable close to a compatible terminal. The technology behind it is called NFC – Near Field Communication.

  • Works with contactless plastic cards
  • Works with mobile wallets (Apple Pay, Google Pay, etc.)
  • Works with wearables (watches, rings, bands)
  • Uses the same global card networks (Visa, Mastercard, etc.)

How a tap transaction actually flows

1. Card or device

Your card or wallet shares a one-time cryptogram instead of your full card number.

2. Terminal

The merchant terminal reads the NFC signal and sends the encrypted data to the acquirer.

3. Network & issuer

The card network and issuer check funds, risk and fraud signals in milliseconds.

4. Approval

The terminal shows approved/declined. For you it feels like a simple “tap & go”.

Card vs wallet vs wearable

Form factor Typical usage Security layer
Contactless plastic card Everyday in-store purchases Chip + NFC, often no PIN below limit
Mobile wallet In-store + in-app Device unlock (PIN/biometrics) + tokenized card number
Wearable Transit, quick checkouts, sports Tokenized card + device-level security

Limits, PIN and risk

Many countries and issuers set a limit for how much you can tap without entering a PIN. Above those limits, the terminal will ask for a PIN or signature just like a chip transaction.

  • Small everyday purchases may not require a PIN
  • Random security checks can ask for chip+PIN even below the limit
  • Lost or stolen card policies vary by issuer
  • Mobile wallets often have stronger security than physical cards

Security best practices with tap

  • Lock your phone and wearables with PIN or biometrics
  • Enable transaction alerts in your banking app
  • Remove unused cards from your wallet
  • Report lost cards and devices immediately
